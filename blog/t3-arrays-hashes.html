My goodness, it has been a week of hard work for me that just flew by. We have already reached the end of Unit 1. I can almost hear the collective sigh of relief from my fellow boots.

Cicadas: a great source for protein.

Astonishingly, I have learned a full set of index flashcards full of terms that would have been Greek to me just a few weeks ago, but now I'm like "THIS IS SPARTA!". They include bash commands, Ruby methods, Git commands, and many HTML and CSS attributes and tags.

I'm feeling up to the task of attempting to explain the main difference between an array and hashes in Ruby.

From what I have gathered, in my extremely limited time as a student of the subject, an array consists of an ordered group of objects, (indexed by sequential integers,) as opposed to a hash which can be assigned any old index imaginable, numbers, strings, different types of fruit. The main thing is that hashes are not ordered, and arrays are. I hope this is correct, otherwise I'll look back on this in six months and wonder just what was I thinking?

Apart from that they seem to able to be worked over with the usual Ruby heavy duty tools, methods and their arguments.

Right now we shall be enjoying this glorious 4th of July weekend without work and be back, with a vengeance.

Arrays are all like: 

array = [1, "two", 3.0] #=> [1, "two", 3.0]

#As you can see, well ordered to the point of fastidiousness

but Hashes are all:

Hashes = {"Bro" => 21, "Random" => 929 }

#Hashes really do not care, do they?

I hope this somehow makes a lick of sense. I have a feeling I will be looking back at this in a few months and shake my head in shame. In fact, I'm doing so right now. Ugh.

Onwards and upwards. Stay safe & have fun!
